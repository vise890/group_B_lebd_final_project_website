Geometry development

As we said, the design of the geometry has been one of our first steps to start the project, but working together we founded that some changes or adjustments were needed.

First geometry: 

-The rooms distributed on two overlapping floors, in order to not waste the heating of the ground floor, but instead using it to contribute to the heating also of the first floor.

-Living room, kitchen and two bedrooms exposed to south.

-Behind this, another block (in blue in the following 3D model), height just one floor and with the flat roof, for an utility room and the garage.

-Main roof: 34 degrees sloping roof covered by PVs (the angle of inclination is due to the best yeld of PV in the zone analysed, as it’s explained in the Photovoltaic panel’s analysis)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

While we were calculating the surface available for the PV we realized that maybe another solution could have been more efficient, so we designed a new geometry that gave us a larger roof surface to increase the PVs area.

Second geometry:

-  The dwelling is still composed of two floors with the same surface as before, but in this solution they are not completly overlapping. This decision was taken trying to increase the roof surface available for the PVs.

- The the interior layout varies slightly compared to the previous; living room, kitchen and two bedrooms are still on the south facade.

- With this second geometry two roof’s layout have been analysed: one with 34 degrees sloping main roof and 20 degrees sloping small roof (figure at bottom left), the other solution with 34 degrees sloping main roof and flat small roof (figure at bottom right)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

To decide which was the best configuration, we made some calculations for the PVs and the most profitable was found to be the one with both the roofs inclined (solution on the left).

*the values ​​of the angles of inclination of the roofs have been established depending on the angle of better yield; for small roof, the angle has been reduced to allow the opening of windows in the adjacent wall.

More descriptions of the appearance and internal layout of the chosen geometry could be found in the following pages: plans and 3D model
