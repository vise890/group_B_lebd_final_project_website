Materials

The choosing of the materials has been a very important part of the project because we had to respect all the PassivHaus standars for insulation, manage to find materials suitable for our idea of the design and try to use, as much as possible, local materials.

Insulating elements of good quality and with good U-values, are not enough to ensure a good behavior of the building, also airtightness must be adjusted (air leakage through unwanted gaps must be less than 0.6 times the house volume per hour) and ventilation has to be controlled (30m³ of fresh, healty air per person per hour). A PassivHaus building has also to be thermal bridge free, or, practically, the value has to be less than 0.01W/mK.

To ensure proper insulation of the house we took in analyzing the following elements:

WALLS

(PassivHaus standard for walls is: U-value < 0.15W/m²K). We chose the NBT PAVACLAD system* with a timber cladding and we achieved a U-value = 0.14W/m²K

-Cladding system
-Vertical battens 40×40 mm
-Insulating layer: NBT PAVATHERM-PLUS 80 mm
-Stud and NBT PAVAFLEX
-OSB 12/15 mm
-Service void 50 mm (for improved thermal performance)
-Plasterboard 12.5mm + paint

Cladding system: we chose a timber external cladding because it’s a natural homegrown material (such as cedar or larch), the manufacturing process is short and the average of cycle of life around 25 years.

ROOF

(PassivHaus standard for roof is: U-value < 0.15W/m²K).We achieve for the roof a U-value = 0.13 W/m²K using a pitched roof with insulation between and on top of the structural members provided by NBT*.

-Roofing
-Counter battens
-Insulating layer: NBT PAVATHERM-PLUS 80 mm
-OSB 9/12 mm
-Rafters 150 mm
-Airtightness membrane
-Service void 25 mm (for improved thermal performance)
-Plasterboard 12.5mm + paint

Actually our roof is not going to look like this because over the roofing we have the Photo-voltaic panels, but all the other layers the same.

WINDOWS and DOORS

(PassivHaus standard for windows is: U-value < 0.8W/m²K and solar energy transmittance factor: at least 50%). We selected to use ENERsign** windows to get a U-value = 0.65 W/m²K and Frostkorken** external door achieving a  U-value = 0.72 W/m²K.

-Triple glazing with two low-e coatings (filled with argon or krypton gas)
-warm edge spacers
-frames with increased insulation levels
This kind of windows reduces heat losses (the total heat loss of  a PH window is only half as high as compared to a normal new window), it reduces thermal bridges and also condensation. There is no cold radiation coming from the window or the frame, so the thermal comfort is guaranteed( the interior surface temperature of such a window will exceed 17 °C as required by PassivHaus standards)
ENERsign** window reduces the noise and it has a cycle of life of about 30 years, requiring only little maintenance.

 

*all the info about these materials were taken from www.natural-building.co.uk/ . NBT is a UK firm, we selected it trying to use local products.

**all the info about these windows were taken from www.greenbuildingstore.co.uk/ . Green building store provides products for low energy buildings in the UK.
